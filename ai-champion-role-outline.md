# AI Champion Role Outline

## Purpose of the role

An AI Champion helps teams adopt AI in a practical, responsible, and repeatable way. The role is not about being the smartest technical person in the room. It is about helping others use AI well, ask better questions, and work through change with confidence.

## Core responsibilities

- Demonstrate useful AI workflows to the team
- Share practical examples and prompt patterns
- Help surface adoption blockers and workflow issues
- Encourage responsible use and good judgment
- Support peer learning and team confidence

## What an AI Champion is not

- Not the sole owner of AI strategy
- Not the final approver for every use case
- Not a replacement for governance or leadership
- Not just a power user showing off tools

## Good signs of success

- More team members use AI in repeatable ways
- People know where AI helps and where human review is needed
- Useful examples are shared across the team
- Adoption becomes more consistent and less ad hoc

## Questions to define the role internally

- Which teams need champions first?
- How much time should champions spend on this role?
- What support will they receive?
- How will success be measured?
- How will champions connect back to leadership or governance?
