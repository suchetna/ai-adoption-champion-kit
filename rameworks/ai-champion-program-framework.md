# AI Champion Program Framework

This framework helps organizations build an internal network of AI champions who support adoption, good practice, and practical experimentation.

## 1. Purpose

Questions:
- Why does the organization need AI champions?
- What adoption problem are they solving?
- What outcomes should the program support?

Strong signals:
- The program has a clear purpose
- Champions are tied to adoption goals
- Expectations are practical

## 2. Champion profile

Questions:
- Who should become a champion?
- What behaviors matter most?
- Do they need influence, curiosity, credibility, or all three?

Strong signals:
- Champions are respected by peers
- They are willing to test and share
- They are not chosen only by title

## 3. Role and responsibilities

Questions:
- What are champions expected to do?
- How much time should they spend?
- What is not part of the role?

Strong signals:
- Responsibilities are specific
- The role is realistic
- Boundaries are clear

## 4. Support and enablement

Questions:
- What training will champions receive?
- What resources or office hours will support them?
- How will they share examples and questions?

Strong signals:
- Champions are equipped, not just named
- There is a clear support system
- Good practices can spread

## 5. Feedback and learning

Questions:
- How will champions share what they are seeing?
- How will issues, needs, and ideas flow back to leadership?
- How will the program improve over time?

Strong signals:
- Feedback loops are clear
- Champions influence improvement
- Learning is documented and reused

## 6. Recognition and continuity

Questions:
- How will the work of champions be recognized?
- How will the program stay active over time?
- What happens if champions leave or rotate out?

Strong signals:
- The role is visible and valued
- Continuity is planned
- The program can scale
